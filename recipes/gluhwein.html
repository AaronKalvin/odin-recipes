Gluhwein Recipe

Description

Gluhwein is a traditional German beverage, typically enjoyed during the winter months, especially around Christmas. It is a hot, spiced wine, usually made with red wine, various mulling spices like cinnamon sticks, cloves, star anise, citrus, and sugar, creating a warm aromatic and sweet flavor.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup water
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 1 (3 inch) cinnamon stick
  • 2 medium oranges
  • 10 cloves
  • 1 bottle of red wine

Steps

  1. Step 1: Gather all Ingredients
  2. Step 2: combine water, sugar, and cinnamon stick in a saucepan; bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low.
  3. Step 3: Cut one orange in half; squeeze orange halves over the sugar mixture to relase their juice. Push 5 cloves into the outside of each orange pee; place peels into the simmering mixture. Continue to simmer over the lowest heat until thick and syrupy, 20 to 30 minuts.
  4. Step 4: While the mixture is simmering, slice remaining orange and set aside for serving.
  5. Step 5: When the mixture is finished simmering, stir in wine and heat until steaming but not simmering, 3 to 5 minutes. Discard orange peels and cinnamon stick.
  6. Step 6: Ladle into mugs and garnish with orange slices. Prost!
